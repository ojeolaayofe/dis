Managing End-of-Life Infrastructure

It is no news that every business must have infrastructure put in place for the smooth running of operations. However, being equipped with the right infrastructure may not guarantee total sustainability and maximum productivity if management is not in place. Perhaps, the most critical concern is how you manage end-of-life infrastructure. Thus, this article presents a laconic explanation of how to manage end-of-life infrastructure to guarantee business continuity. Enjoy the read.

What Do I Mean By End-of-Life Infrastructure?

In this context, I am referring to IT Infrastructures which means the combination of the hardware, software, pieces of equipment, facilities, and other components that aid in the successful delivery of the service operations offered by a business or a firm. In simpler terms, IT Infrastructures are the foundational resources or say a framework that keeps the business in operation. They may otherwise be called supportive systems because they control the flow of storage, processing, and analysis of the business data & insights. Some of these IT infrastructures may include servers, cloud storage facilities, network hubs, data centres, workstations, potential software, routers, cables, Internet facilities, hardware like printing machines, connecting devices, network security devices, Operating systems, and even Computer systems.

So, How Do You Manage End-of-Life Infrastructure?

Having understood the essence and the value of Infrastructures, It is required to now understand how to manage these Infrastructures at their end of life. Several businesses have suffered the implications of not paying attention to the management of these infrastructures at their end-of-life. Please do not miss the following points:

  • Prepare a plan that helps you manage the lifecycle of these infrastructures.
  • Continuous backup of OS and data
  • Conduct frequent inspections of infrastructure.
  • Take inventory of the entire business assets.
  • Monitor the performance of devices constantly.
  • Maintain constant security protocols.
  • Review the ethical and standard compliance occasionally.
  • Hire a professional to effectively manage your infrastructure.

Emphasising the last point above, many businesses have found themselves in hot water over their negligence in managing end-of-life infrastructures. To avoid being in the same tragedy, it is highly recommended to partner or hire professionals who are conversant in that area.
